\begin{abstract}

\noindent \textbf{Abstract:} This report investigates the Kosterlitz-Thouless phase transition in the two-dimensional XY model through Monte Carlo simulations employing the Metropolis and Wolff cluster algorithms. Key observables---energy, specific heat, magnetization, and susceptibility---are analyzed across lattice sizes $L=8$ to $256$ and temperatures $T \in [0,2]$. The Wolff algorithm demonstrates superior efficiency, with autocorrelation times reduced by up to two orders of magnitude compared to Metropolis, mitigating critical slowing down near $T_c$. Finite-size scaling of susceptibility peaks yields $T_c = 0.884 \pm 0.026$ and correlation length exponent $\nu = 0.562 \pm 0.057$, consistent with theoretical values within uncertainties. Data collapse confirms scaling behavior, highlighting the topological nature of the KT transition characterized by vortex unbinding \cite{Hasenbusch2008}.
\end{abstract}
